# Andy Dev Dan

Host of a YouTube channel focused on agentic engineering for senior developers, owner of *agenticengineer.com*. Sells two courses — **Tactical Agent Coding** and **Agent Horizon** — and promotes a third-party harness tool ("PI agent harness," credited to "Mario") that he uses as the substrate for most of his examples. The third primary practitioner voice in this wiki, alongside [[simon-willison]] and [[andrej-karpathy]].

## Synthesis

A few features of Andy Dev Dan's perspective worth holding when reading the pages he's cited on:

- **Vantage: the individual senior engineer at the leading edge.** Where [[simon-willison]] writes a practitioner's manual that scales down to any team and [[andrej-karpathy]] names paradigms for the whole field, Andy writes for the **solo engineer who wants to be in the top 2%**. His pillars target the gap between *high- and low-performing individual practitioners*, not between organizations (that's [[kumar-ramagopal]]'s vantage). He claims to build "one new custom agent harness every day" — credibly or not, the framing is *individual maximization*.

- **Practitioner-influencer with a course business.** This is a flag, not a dismissal. Andy ships code (his videos demo working systems) and is clearly hands-on, but the talks are also sales surfaces for *Tactical Agent Coding*, *Agent Horizon*, and the PI harness. Several headline claims in [[2026-05-28-andy-dev-dan-five-pillars]] are sales-shaped: *"top 500 companies all building software factories,"* *"90% of agent cron jobs are dead useless,"* *"by end of 2026 agentic engineering will be the default."* Treat his sharper assertions as practitioner conjecture, not reported facts.

- **Substrate-maximalist.** His strongest claim: ***"Models matter less and less. What matters is the systems you place around your agents."*** This is a sharper version of [[simon-willison|Willison's]] harness-and-tools-in-a-loop framing and Karpathy's *"define the context / tools / feedback loop / guardrails"* recipe. For Andy, **the substrate — harness + factory + agentic-access surface — is the moat.** This is unusual enough to track as future sources arrive; some will likely push back. **Partial corroboration** has since arrived from [[cole-medin]], an independent YouTuber who reaches the same harness-primacy conclusion with a cleaner structural model — see [[agent-harness-engineering]]. Two converging practitioner voices strengthen the claim, though both still have commercial offerings (Andy's courses; Cole's Archon), so it isn't yet disinterested confirmation.

- **Vocabulary contributions.** Several terms come into the wiki from Andy and don't appear in Willison, Karpathy, or Kumar & Ramagopal: **AFK agents** (always-on agents as the *output* of getting tokeconomics right), **software factory** / **dark factory** / **ADWs** (AI Developer Workflows), **ZTE** (Zero Touch Engineering), **token tax**, **token arbitrage / tokeconomics**, **specialization is the moat**. Several of these now anchor their own topic pages in the wiki ([[software-factory]], [[tokeconomics]], [[agent-harness-engineering]]).

- **Style: declarative, repetitive, motivational.** The video is explicitly framed as a "raw note to myself" recorded after a two-week break. The structure repeats phrases ("whoever controls the X controls your results") and goes for emphasis over nuance. The substance is real but the rhetoric is influencer-shaped.

A useful posture for reading Andy on this wiki: he names patterns the others haven't, and his pillars are usable as *prescriptions*, but verify his sweeping claims against [[simon-willison|Willison's]] more carefully-bounded practitioner observations and [[andrej-karpathy|Karpathy's]] analytic frames.

## Open questions

- The PI agent harness is referenced repeatedly but the wiki doesn't yet have a primary source on it. Ingesting docs or a primary write-up of PI would let [[agent-harness-engineering]] move from 1 source to 2.
- *Tactical Agent Coding* is Andy's course. Does it cover ground beyond the five-pillars video? Worth ingesting a syllabus or a substantive sample if available.
- How do Andy's claims about the *individual* engineer's ROI compose with Kumar & Ramagopal's *organization*-scale numbers (93% debug time reduction, 65% dev time reduction)? Both groups claim massive multipliers but from different vantages.
