# Geoffrey Huntley

Credited as the **creator of the [[ralph-loop|Ralph loop]]** and, per [[cole-medin]], *"one of the pioneers"* of automating the stringing-together of many coding-agent sessions. Enters the wiki as a **secondhand mention only** — no primary source from Huntley himself has been ingested yet.

## Synthesis

Everything the wiki currently records about Huntley comes through [[cole-medin]]'s description in [[2026-05-29-cole-medin-harness-engineering]]: Huntley originated the **Ralph loop**, a minimal, script-based pattern (bash or Python) that runs coding-agent sessions in a `while` loop against a large scope of work until a `done.txt`-style completion marker is written. Cole presents it as "one of the first examples" showing, in a very basic sense, how to automate orchestration — the foundational technique underneath the more elaborate [[software-factory|software-factory]] / [[agent-harness-engineering|harness-engineering]] orchestration patterns.

Because the wiki has only the secondhand account, this page is deliberately thin. The substance of the technique lives on [[ralph-loop]]; this page exists so the originator is attributed and not orphaned.
